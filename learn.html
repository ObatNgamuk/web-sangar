Expert Tips

The Golden Rules

1:16

Golden Ratio

Start with 1 gram of coffee for every 16 grams of water. Adjust to taste based on your preferred strength and the specific origin characteristics.

93°C

Water Temperature

Ideal brewing temperature is between 90-96°C (195-205°F). Lighter roasts benefit from hotter water, darker roasts from slightly cooler.

Fresh

Grind Fresh

Always grind your beans immediately before brewing. Coffee begins losing flavor within minutes of grinding. Invest in a quality burr grinder.

Understanding Extraction

The Science of Perfect Coffee

Coffee extraction is the process of dissolving soluble compounds from roasted coffee grounds into water. The balance of these compounds determines the flavor profile of your cup.

Under-extraction results in sour, acidic flavors, while over-extraction produces bitter, harsh notes. The goal is to find the sweet spot where sugars, acids, and bitter compounds are in perfect harmony.

Grind Size Matters

Finer grinds extract faster, coarser grinds slower. Match your grind to your brew method.

Water Quality

Use filtered water for the cleanest taste. Hard water can mask delicate flavors.

Bloom Phase

Pre-wet grounds for 30-45 seconds to release CO2 and ensure even extraction.
